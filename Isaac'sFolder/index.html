Chest Day Workout

Warm-up

  • 5–10 minutes light cardio
  • 2–3 light sets of bench press

Main lifts

  • Flat Barbell Bench Press – 4 sets of 5–8 reps
  • Incline Dumbbell Press – 3 sets of 8–10 reps

Accessory work

  • Dumbbell Flys – 3 sets of 10–12 reps
  • Chest Dips (lean forward) – 3 sets to failure

Finisher

  • Cable Crossovers – 3 sets of 12–15 reps
  • Push-ups – 2 sets to failure
Rest time: 60–90 seconds between sets (longer for heavy bench sets)
Focus: Controlled movement, full range of motion, squeeze chest at the top of every rep

Leg Day Workout

Warm-up

  • 5–10 minutes light cardio (bike, treadmill, or incline walk)
  • Bodyweight squats + lunges to activate legs

Main lifts

  • Barbell Back Squat – 4 sets of 5–8 reps
  • Romanian Deadlift – 3 sets of 6–10 reps

Quad focus

  • Leg Press – 3 sets of 10–12 reps
  • Walking Lunges – 3 sets of 10–12 steps each leg

Hamstrings & glutes

  • Lying or Seated Leg Curl – 3 sets of 10–12 reps
  • Hip Thrusts – 3 sets of 8–12 reps

Calves

  • Standing Calf Raises – 4 sets of 12–15 reps
  • Seated Calf Raises – 3 sets of 12–15 reps

Finisher (optional)

  • Bodyweight squats – 2 sets to failure
Rest time: 60–90 seconds between sets (2–3 min for heavy squats)
Focus: Deep range of motion, controlled tempo, drive through heels and stay consistent with form

Design

Style Sheet

1. The Layout Blocks (Three New Divs)

Instead of everything flowing in one long line, we will use three specific containers to group your information:

  • The Navigation Sidebar: A vertical or top-aligned box that holds your menu buttons, keeping them separate from the workout details.
  • The Main Content Stage: A large, centered “glass” container that holds the specific workout text or reports you are viewing.
  • The Focus Callout: A specialized box at the bottom of the page designed to highlight the “Note” and “Rest Time” sections.

2. The Custom Stylesheet Design

Your scratch-built stylesheet will define the look of the site through these four key rules:

Font family

The site will use a modern sans-serif system font. This removes the “old web” look and makes the text look like a professional fitness app—sharp, clean, and easy to read while moving.

Font color

We will use a high-contrast palette. The background will be a dark ink color, while the main text will be a soft ghost white. This ensures you can read your reps and sets even in a bright gym environment.

Header styling

All main titles will be styled in bold uppercase with a blue gradient. This makes the name of the workout (like “CHEST DAY”) stand out as the most important piece of information on the screen.

Container borders

The containers will use glow borders. Instead of plain black lines, the edges of your content boxes will have a thin, translucent blue glow to give the site a high-tech, “cyber-gym” feel.

3. Visual Summary

The final result will look like a structured dashboard. The Sidebar handles the navigation, the Main Stage displays your exercises in clean white text, and the Focus Callout uses a bright accent color to make sure you don't forget to rest between sets.
